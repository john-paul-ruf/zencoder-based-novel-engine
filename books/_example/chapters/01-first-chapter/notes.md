## Author Notes — Chapter 1

**Voice decisions:**
- Embedded Cole's counting obsession into the prose rhythm itself—short sentences ("Cole read.") breaking up longer ones to mirror how his mind works: methodical processing interrupted by sharp clarity.
- "Performing the social shapes of talking" — inhabiting the character's observational distance; he sees people as systems to evaluate.
- Minimal dialogue (one line from Polk, kept generic). Cole's voice is interior; he doesn't perform for others.
- Embedded technical reading (the charter quotes) within interiority—exposition delivered through Cole's mental process, not as summary.
- Emotional gut-punch reserved for the Lily section: sentence rhythm shifts from staccato fragments ("He'd said yes. Meant it.") to longer vulnerable clauses. Physical details (the colored pencil horse, the table, the recycle bin) grounding abstraction.
- Lily section expanded to include career collapse (contract positions paying less each quarter), shrinking apartment (two-bedroom → studio → rented room), and visit frequency declining (weekly → quarterly) per outline beats.

**Structural choices:**
- Opening moves from external observation (room, people) to Cole's singular focus (reading) to his interior calculation—funneling outward → inward → the dark decision.
- Charter reading is structurally the spine: moves from Section 1 → Sections 2-5 → Section 6 → appendices. Each layer reveals more.
- Garrett Polk appears twice: once observed in waiting room (the man "not reading"), once in the corridor post-signing. Corridor interaction kept minimal—Polk speaks one generic line ("Hell of a thing"), does NOT reference the contract or clause 9.4.2. Cole reads Polk's body language and concludes he hasn't read the charter. This preserves Ch 2 outline continuity where Polk "clearly hasn't read the contract."
- Final paragraph is Cole's internal understanding of his own choice: frontier vs. margin.
