## Author Notes — Chapter 4

**Voice decisions:**
- Deep interiority drives Beat 2 entirely. Cole's reading of Polk is the chapter's richest interior passage — he doesn't just observe Polk's resistance, he diagnoses it. "Polk wasn't seeing a water protocol. He was seeing a trap dressed up as salvation." This is Cole's analytical mode applied to human damage rather than logistics. Consistent with Ch 1's face-counting and Ch 2's assessment of the transit group.
- Polk's dialogue stripped to essentials. His smartest move is rhetorical: "Same survey that said the shelters would handle the cold. Same survey that said the UV wasn't a problem." He connects dots fast, which matches story bible ("Smarter than he performs — catches gaps in logic that others miss"). His anger is visceral, not performative — rooted in the specific wound of having trusted systems before.
- Dana's voice kept minimal. She reports data, not conclusions. "The shifts aren't noise. They're signal." She does NOT yet have the orbital mechanics theory — that's her Ch 8 breakthrough. Here she's gathering, not modeling.
- Haruki's dialogue is the chapter's pivot: "On Earth, we've had ten thousand years to learn the calendar. Here, we don't even know the length of a season." Gentle, precise, devastating. Consistent with story bible ("Asks 'How are you?' and means it diagnostically").
- "Still photograph" metaphor rendered as Cole's synthesis in interiority, not attributed to another character. Haruki's seasonal analogy provides the raw material; Cole builds the insight.

**Structural choices:**
- Chapter organized as four clear systems (water, food, shelter, perimeter) followed by their social consequences. The systems-list structure mirrors Cole's mind — systematic, sequential, logistical. Then the Polk confrontation breaks the systems apart emotionally.
- Scene break (---) between Polk section and Suri incident creates two distinct movements: the social problem (Beat 2) and the biological problem (Beat 3). Both end at the same conclusion — the planet's rules aren't what they were told.
- Meeting kept informal (gathering at common area, not formal assembly) to distinguish from Ch 5's logistics briefing, which is the escalation after actual deaths. Ch 4's meeting is about protocol; Ch 5's is about the count.
- Ending returns to the chapter title. "The planet had rules. They just hadn't finished learning them yet." Quiet close. No over-explanation.
