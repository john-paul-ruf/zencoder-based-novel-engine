# Notes — Chapter 01: The Keeper

## Author Note (Verity)

### Voice Decisions
- Established the sentence rhythm: medium-length baseline with short fragments for emotional beats ("There were worse fates." / "She would not forget. She would also not call."). The longer sentences carry landscape and interiority; the short ones land the punches.
- Kept vocabulary mid-literary — "pewter," "Fresnel," "flagstone" — specific but not showy. One metaphor per movement: the lighthouse as a "slow, deliberate eye," the stone passageway as a "throat."
- Maeve's internal voice is denser than her spoken voice. She speaks in short deflections ("It can." / "Patience. And lower expectations."). Her thoughts run longer, circling what she doesn't want to face.

### Structural Choices
- Split the chapter with a scene break: the first half establishes Maeve's solitude and the intrusion of the news. The second half is the arrival. This gives the first section room to breathe as a portrait before the inciting element lands.
- Ended on Maeve's POV of Callum's arrival rather than saving it for his chapter. This plants the reader inside Maeve's knowledge gap — we know she recognizes him before he opens his mouth. Chapter 2 will replay this arrival from his side, ignorant of what she knows.
- The recognition reveal happens fast — no delayed mystery. The reader should be inside the asymmetry from the first meeting. The tension isn't "will she figure out who he is" — it's "what will she do with what she knows."

### Passages Flagged
- The "arithmetic of solitude" line — this is a narrator-voice moment that may be too writerly for the established register. Monitor whether this level of metaphor is sustainable or needs to be reserved for rarer moments.
- "Like a coin into deep water" — first use of this kind of physical-sensation-as-metaphor for her recognition. May want to track how physical the recognition language stays vs. becoming cognitive.
